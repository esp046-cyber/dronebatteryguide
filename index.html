What is a Drone Battery?

Drone batteries are rechargeable power sources that provide energy for flight. Most modern drones use Lithium Polymer (LiPo) batteries due to their high energy density and light weight.

Quick Tip

Higher mAh = Longer flight time. Higher C-rating = More power available. More cells (S) = Higher voltage and speed.

LiPo (Lithium Polymer)

Most Common Type

  • High energy density and lightweight
  • Flexible shapes and sizes
  • Requires careful handling and storage
  • Nominal voltage: 3.7V per cell
  • Full charge: 4.2V per cell

Li-ion (Lithium Ion)

Consumer Drones

  • Safer and more stable than LiPo
  • Lower maintenance requirements
  • Slightly heavier for same capacity
  • Common in DJI and other consumer drones

LiHV (Lithium High Voltage)

Performance Racing

  • Higher voltage per cell (3.8V nominal, 4.35V full)
  • More power and longer flight time
  • Requires compatible charger
  • Popular in FPV racing drones

  • Charge on flammable surfaces

  • Never short circuit terminals
  • Keep away from water and moisture
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
  • Stop using if battery swells or gets hot

Signs of Damaged Battery

  • Swelling or puffing (puffy pack)
  • Physical damage to casing
  • Exposed cells or wires
  • Excessive heat during use
  • Voltage imbalance between cells (>0.1V)
  • Rapid voltage drop during flight

If any signs present: STOP USING IMMEDIATELY

Emergency: Battery Fire

  • Move battery outdoors if safe to do so
  • Do NOT use water (spreads lithium fire)
  • Use sand, dirt, or Class D extinguisher
  • Let it burn out in safe location if necessary
  • Call emergency services for large fires

  • Don't land at critically low voltage

  • Store in LiPo bag or ammo can
  • Check voltage every 2-3 months

  • Keep in temperature-stable environment

  • Don't store fully charged
  • Keep battery cool during use
  • Use appropriate C-rating for your drone
  • Balance charge every time
  • Cycle batteries (rotate use among packs)

Battery Won't Charge

Possible Causes:

  • Charger not detecting battery - check connections
  • Wrong charge settings - verify cell count
  • Damaged cells - if beyond repair, dispose properly

Battery Gets Hot During Flight

Solutions:

  • C-rating too low for your drone - upgrade battery
  • Battery aging - consider replacement
  • Excessive current draw - check drone components
  • Hot weather - allow cooling time between flights

Short Flight Time

Check These:

  • Battery capacity degraded - normal with age
  • Not fully charged - check charger
  • Voltage sag under load - internal resistance increased
  • Cold weather - reduces capacity temporarily
  • Drone issues - increased power consumption

Cell Voltage Imbalance

Difference >0.1V between cells:

  • Run balance charge cycle
  • If persists after 2-3 cycles, retire battery
  • Indicates cell degradation
  • Can cause failure during flight

Swollen Battery

This is SERIOUS:

  • Stop using immediately
  • Do NOT charge
  • Discharge to 0V in salt water bath
  • Dispose at battery recycling center
  • Never throw in regular trash

Safe Battery Disposal

Step-by-step:

  • Discharge battery to 0V (salt water method or discharge tool)
  • Tape terminals to prevent shorts
  • Take to electronics recycling center
  • Many hobby shops accept old batteries
  • Check local hazardous waste facilities

Salt Water Discharge Method

Mix salt water, place battery fully submerged for 24 hours. This safely discharges battery to 0V for disposal. Do this outdoors in non-metal container.

Voltage Sag Explained

Understanding voltage drop:

  • Higher IR = more voltage sag
  • Affects performance and flight time
  • Example: 16.8V battery sags to 14.5V under full throttle

Parallel vs. Series Configuration

Series (S):

  • Voltage adds up (3S = 11.1V, 4S = 14.8V)
  • Capacity stays same
  • More cells = more power and speed

Parallel (P):

  • Capacity adds up (2P = double mAh)
  • Voltage stays same
  • Used in larger battery packs
  • Example: 4S2P = 4 cells series, 2 parallel = double capacity at 14.8V

How LiPo Batteries Work

Why 3.7V Per Cell?

  • Different chemistries have different voltages

Understanding C-Rating Science

What C-rating actually means:

  • C-rating = multiple of capacity
  • 1C = discharge at capacity rate (1500mAh = 1.5A)
  • 75C = 75√ó capacity (1500mAh = 112.5A)
  • Limited by: electrode surface area, electrolyte conductivity
  • Higher C = thicker electrodes = less capacity
  • Trade-off: high C-rating OR high capacity, rarely both

Why Batteries Puff/Swell

Swelling mechanism:

  • Gas generation from electrolyte breakdown
  • Caused by: overcharge, overdischarge, heat, age
  • Gases: CO2, CO, hydrocarbons
  • Pressure builds up inside pouch
  • Irreversible damage to cell structure
  • Swollen battery = permanent damage, retire immediately

Series vs Parallel Science

Why series adds voltage:

  • Series: Cells connected + to - to + to -
  • Voltages add: 3.7V + 3.7V + 3.7V = 11.1V (3S)
  • Capacity stays same
  • Current flows through all cells equally

Why parallel adds capacity:

  • Parallel: All + together, all - together
  • Voltage stays same: 3.7V
  • Capacity adds: 1500mAh + 1500mAh = 3000mAh
  • Current splits between cells
